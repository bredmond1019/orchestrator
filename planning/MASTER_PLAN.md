## The Goal, Stated Plainly

The goal is **expertise** in AI Engineering, Agentic Engineering, Harness Engineering, and the surrounding architecture — and a **solo contracting practice** built from that expertise.

Not a studio. Not a venture-backed product. Not a grind toward some future state where life gets good. A practice that funds a good life *now*: working on interesting technical problems, controlling the schedule, and having real time and energy left for music and family.

The target steady state is roughly **20 billable hours per week** — enough for a strong income at senior AI engineering rates, not so much that it crowds out everything else. Getting there means building genuine expertise first, because expertise is what commands the rates and the client relationships that make that kind of practice sustainable. *(See DECISIONS D1, D26.)*

### What "ready to contract" means

You are ready to pitch your first paid engagement when:

> **You can walk into an SMB you've never seen and name three automatable workflows in 30 minutes, and explain how you'd build each one on your own infrastructure.**

That's it. You don't need the full project library done. You don't need a polished product. You need to be able to scope work accurately and deliver it reliably — and the through-line of case studies you already have (Dashboard, Helpscout, AI Scribe) means you're closer to this bar than a "starting out" framing suggests.

If you hit the checkpoint and still hesitate, send one diagnostic offer anyway. The hesitation is almost never about readiness.

### The through-line you're building on

You are not pivoting into AI. You have been building adopted, practical AI-and-automation tools for years. State that story as one arc, because it's true:

> **Internal Support Dashboard** (saw an unmet need, built it solo, 100+ people adopted it and still use it daily) → **Helpscout Support Automation** (saw a support bottleneck, built RAG-based automation to fix it, solo, in production) → **AI Scribe** (contributed heavily to production healthcare AI at scale) → **early Claude Code / Aider experiments** (was early, shipped fast, learned where fast AI-building goes wrong) → **now**: building production-grade agentic systems and taking on contract work that puts them to use.

That arc serves a contracting pitch and a senior job interview equally. Lead with it.

## The Contracting Practice

### What you're selling

Three tiers of engagement, each naturally flowing into the next:

**Paid diagnostic ($1,000–2,000, 1–2 weeks):** Map a client's workflows, identify automation opportunities, deliver a scoped plan with one quick win built and working. This is your foot in the door and your proposal generator (Project C) in action.

**Project work ($5,000–15,000 fixed price):** Build a specific system — a RAG pipeline over their documents, an AI workflow automation, a custom agent. This is where Projects D, B, and C pay off directly.

**Retainer ($2,000–5,000/month ongoing):** Maintenance, iteration, new features for a system you've already built. This is the stable base. One or two good retainers at this rate, combined with occasional project work, is the whole practice.

### The services you can offer right now (post Phase 0 + Project D)

- **RAG systems over business documents** — the most common SMB ask. Your Helpscout production work makes this a proven competence, not an aspiration. Project D is the clean portfolio rebuild.
- **AI workflow automation** — custom pipelines using your orchestration framework. Faster to build and more robust than n8n/Zapier for anything with real complexity.
- **AI chatbots and assistants** — WhatsApp bots, internal Q&A systems, customer support automation.
- **Automation consulting / diagnostics** — scoping engagements where you map workflows and propose what to build.

### Where to find work

**Upwork** — fastest path to first clients. Position around production AI pipelines and RAG systems, not "n8n expert." Your Helpscout case study is the lead. Target the $3–10K fixed-price RAG chatbot and AI automation gigs.

**Toptal** — higher rates, rigorous vetting, worth applying once Project D is shipped. The production track record (Dashboard, Helpscout, AI Scribe) gives you a real shot. Rates for AI specialists run $100–200/hr.

**LinkedIn** — warm São Paulo relationships, local contracting, and US/EU companies looking for LATAM talent.

**Wellfound** — startup-focused contract roles, good for longer relationships.

**Direct outreach** — your warm leads (the CrossFit gym, the e-commerce seller) are the first two. Research conversations before pitches. The question that opens the most doors: *"What's the most repetitive thing your team does that eats time — and where does knowledge live that only one person knows?"*

### Positioning

You're not competing on no-code Zapier work. You're the person who builds the thing Zapier can't do: production-grade, custom, Python-backed pipelines with real architecture behind them. That's a different buyer and 3–5× the rate.

Your bilingual advantage (PT/EN) is real: it opens both local São Paulo clients and US/EU companies seeking LATAM talent. Use it.

The "senior engineer who works selectively and has a life" positioning is an asset with good clients, not a liability. Clients who've been burned by contractors who overcommit and underdeliver actively want the person who scopes carefully and delivers reliably.

### Block 6.5 — Project H: Model Evaluation & Routing Harness

An offline tool that runs each node against frontier and local models, scores the outputs, and produces empirical per-node routing decisions. Its output bakes into each node's `model_provider` config.

Why this matters for contracting: being able to say "I measured which steps in this pipeline are safe on a local model and which need frontier reasoning, here's the data" is a senior signal that differentiates from people who just hook up the OpenAI API. It also unlocks an honest "runs on your hardware" offer for privacy-conscious clients. *(DECISIONS D8, D19.)*

---

### Block 7 — Project G: Agent Memory System (Episodic → Semantic)

The hardest and most differentiating thing in the plan. Reasoning-first two-stage memory (fast ingest-time extraction + background consolidation), multi-peer entity modeling, confidence decay, contradiction handling.

Why this matters for contracting: it's the thing that takes a RAG system from "search over your docs" to "a system that gets smarter about your business over time." No competitor who's just wrapping an API can build this. It's the technical depth that justifies a retainer relationship — because the system keeps improving.

Read Honcho's source before starting. *(DECISIONS D25.)* Tests ship with it — matter most here, because bad memory output degrades everything downstream silently.

**Visibility:** Your strongest technical content. A deep post and a live demo. Also the demo that upgrades the contracting pitch: show a system that learns across sessions, handles contradictions, and surfaces facts no document ever stated.
